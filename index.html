12-Week Plan (Week-by-Week)

Train 3 days/week (full-body). Optional 4th day for light cardio + mobility. Progress by adding reps, then sets, then difficulty.

Workout Days (A / B / C)

Repeat these each week on non-consecutive days (ex: Mon/Wed/Fri).

Day A

  • Squat (bodyweight)
  • Push-ups (knee or regular)
  • Glute bridge
  • Plank

Day B

  • Reverse lunge
  • Incline push-ups (hands on couch/table)
  • Hip hinge (good morning) or deadlift pattern (backpack)
  • Side plank

Day C

  • Step-ups (stairs/bench)
  • Pike push-ups (or incline)
  • Single-leg glute bridge (or regular)
  • Dead bug

Minimal equipment options: a backpack (books for weight), a sturdy chair, a towel.

How to Progress (Simple Rules)

  • Weeks 1–4: Add reps each week (same sets).
  • Weeks 5–8: Add a set when you hit the top of the rep range.
  • Weeks 9–12: Make moves harder (tempo, pause, backpack weight, harder variation).

Effort guide: stop with 1–2 reps left in the tank on most sets (good form first).

Safety & Recovery (Quick)

  • Warm up 5 minutes (marching in place, arm circles, bodyweight squats).
  • Rest 60–90 seconds between sets.
  • If pain (sharp) shows up, stop and regress the exercise.
  • Sleep and protein matter for muscle growth.

Workout Video Library

Learn proper form with step-by-step video demonstrations for each exercise.

Why Proper Form Matters

Correct exercise form is crucial for building muscle safely and effectively. These videos demonstrate the proper technique for each movement in the 12-week plan. Watch them before your first workout and refer back anytime you need a refresher.

Day A Exercises

Bodyweight Squat

Key points: Feet shoulder-width apart, chest up, knees tracking over toes, sit back like sitting in a chair.

Push-ups (Knee or Regular)

Key points: Hands shoulder-width, body in straight line, lower chest to ground, elbows at 45-degree angle.

Glute Bridge

Key points: Lie on back, feet flat, drive through heels, squeeze glutes at top, keep core tight.

Plank

Key points: Forearms on ground, body straight from head to heels, don't let hips sag, breathe steadily.

Day B Exercises

Reverse Lunge

Key points: Step back with one leg, lower back knee toward ground, keep front knee over ankle, push through front heel.

Incline Push-ups

Key points: Hands on elevated surface (couch/table), body straight, lower chest to surface, maintain form.

Hip Hinge / Good Morning

Key points: Hinge at hips, slight knee bend, keep back straight, push hips back, feel stretch in hamstrings.

Side Plank

Key points: Balance on forearm and side of foot, body in straight line, hips lifted, engage obliques.

Day C Exercises

Step-ups

Key points: Use stairs or bench, drive through heel of elevated leg, stand fully at top, controlled descent.

Pike Push-ups

Key points: Hips high in inverted V position, lower head toward ground between hands, push back up, targets shoulders.

Single-leg Glute Bridge

Key points: One foot on ground, other leg extended, drive through heel, squeeze glute at top, maintain balance.

Dead Bug

Key points: Lie on back, extend opposite arm and leg, keep lower back pressed to floor, move slowly and controlled.

Video Tips

  • Watch each video fully before attempting the exercise
  • Start with easier variations if needed (knee push-ups, regular glute bridge)
  • Practice movements slowly to master form before adding speed or intensity
  • Pause and replay sections if you need clarification on any movement

Definition of Success

Shared measures the "client" and the "team" agree on.

Quantitative Results

  • Strength up (e.g., +50% reps on squats / push-ups)
  • Plank improved (target: 60+ seconds)
  • ≥85% workout completion over 12 weeks

Visible Improvement

  • Progress photos show changes by weeks 8–12
  • Body measurements improve (optional tracking)
  • Better form and control on key movements

Mindset Shift

  • Confidence training independently at home
  • Clear understanding of progression
  • Motivation to continue beyond 12 weeks

Photo Progress

Track your visual transformation with weekly progress photos.

Why Take Progress Photos?

Progress photos are one of the most powerful tools for tracking your transformation. While the scale and measurements are helpful, photos capture changes in muscle definition, posture, and overall physique that numbers can't show. They also provide incredible motivation when you compare week 1 to week 12.

Photo Guidelines

  • Frequency: Take photos at the end of each week (same day/time)
  • Lighting: Use consistent, natural lighting if possible
  • Location: Same spot in your home each time
  • Clothing: Wear fitted clothing or the same outfit each week
  • Poses: Front, side, and back views with relaxed posture
  • Time of day: Morning after waking (consistent hydration state)

What to Look For

  • Increased muscle definition in arms, shoulders, and legs
  • Improved posture and body composition
  • Changes in how clothing fits
  • Subtle improvements week-to-week add up over 12 weeks
  • Compare Week 1 vs Week 4 vs Week 8 vs Week 12

Pro Tip: Create a Photo Timeline

Store your weekly photos in a dedicated folder on your phone. At the end of 12 weeks, create a side-by-side comparison or video montage to see your complete transformation. This visual record is incredibly motivating and helps you appreciate the progress you've made.
